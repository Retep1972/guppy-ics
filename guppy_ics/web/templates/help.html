This page explains how to interpret the results shown after uploading a PCAP file. All analysis is passive and based only on observed network traffic at the capture point.

Guppy ICS does not perform active scanning, probing, or fingerprinting. Devices and communications not visible in the capture will not appear in the results.

üìä Summary

The summary shows how many assets, communications, and events were discovered during analysis.

üß© Assets

Assets represent discovered devices in the network. A single asset may combine multiple identifiers (for example: MAC and IP address) when identity linkage is observed.

  • Label: Best available identifier (station name, IP address, or MAC)
  • Role: Inferred function (PLC, IO device, HMI, client, server)
  • Protocols: Industrial or management protocols the asset participates in
  • Visibility: How confidently the asset was observed (see below)

Asset Visibility

  • Observed (IP) ‚Äì The device was directly observed communicating at the IP layer (TCP or UDP).
  • Observed (L2 only) ‚Äì The device was directly observed at Ethernet level (for example PROFINET IO), but no IP traffic was seen.
  • Inferred ‚Äì The device was not directly observed communicating, but inferred from identity relationships or network structure.

‚ÄúInferred‚Äù does not mean guessed or simulated ‚Äì it indicates limited visibility from the capture location.

üîÅ Communications

Communications describe how assets interact. Each entry represents a semantic communication relationship, not individual packets.

  • Protocol badge: Detected application protocol (e.g. S7, Modbus, OPC UA, SNMP, DNP3)
  • Function: Meaning of the traffic (client ‚Üí server, controls, response, trap)
  • Direction: Source ‚Üí Destination
  • IP and Ports: Shown when IP-based communication was observed

For Layer-2-only protocols (such as PROFINET IO), no IP addresses or ports are shown, because they do not exist on the wire.

Transport and OSI Layers

  • L2: Ethernet-based industrial traffic (e.g. PROFINET RT)
  • L3: Network layer (IP)
  • L4: Transport layer (TCP or UDP)

Guppy ICS prefers application-level protocols over raw transport information to reduce noise and improve clarity.

üó∫Ô∏è Topology

The topology section provides a logical view of the network based on observed communications.

  • Each root node represents a source asset
  • Indented entries show outgoing communication relationships
  • Only the highest meaningful protocol is shown per relationship

The topology reflects observed communication paths, not physical cabling or switch-level topology.

‚ö†Ô∏è Events

Events highlight notable protocol-level observations when supported by a protocol. Examples include:

  • Detection of insecure SNMP versions (v1 / v2c)
  • Notable management or control behaviors

Not all protocols generate events. Absence of events does not imply absence of risk.

üìÑ Firewall Rule Export (CSV)

Guppy ICS can generate firewall rules based on observed IP-based communications.

  • Includes both TCP and UDP traffic
  • Only includes assets directly observed at IP level
  • Uses observed source/destination IPs and ports
  • Excludes Layer-2-only protocols and inferred assets

The firewall CSV represents what was observed in the PCAP. It does not include assumed, inferred, or unseen communication paths. Always validate rules in a test environment before deployment.

üîê Intended Use

  • Gain situational awareness of ICS and OT networks
  • Understand control, monitoring, and management traffic
  • Support firewall and segmentation design
  • Document brownfield industrial environments

Guppy ICS is designed to assist analysis and decision-making. It does not replace engineering validation or safety review.
